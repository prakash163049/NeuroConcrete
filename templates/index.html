Process Overview

How NeuroConcrete Works

From concrete mix parameters to strength prediction in 4 steps

01

Input Mix Parameters

Enter the 8 concrete mix design parameters — either via the precision form or interactive sliders. Choose from presets like M25, M40 or M60 grades for instant exploration.

CementWaterSlagFly Ash+4 more
02

Feature Vector Assembly

Values are validated against training data bounds and assembled into a normalised 8-dimensional feature vector, then sent to the Flask API via JSON.

[
350 , 0 , 0 , 185 …
]
03

Extra Trees Ensemble Inference

300 randomised decision trees each independently predict the compressive strength. Their outputs are averaged to produce a robust, low-variance final prediction.

×300
avg → MPa
04

Result & Grade Classification

The predicted compressive strength (MPa) is returned and classified into a concrete grade (Low/Medium/High/Ultra-High), visualised on an animated gauge with a full mix summary.

Low <20 MPa Medium 20–40 High 40–60 Ultra >60

Why Extra Trees?

vs. Random Forest
Extra Trees Random Forest
Split selection Fully random ✓ Best-of-K
Training speed Faster ✓ Slower
Variance reduction Higher ✓ Moderate
Overfitting risk Lower ✓ Moderate
Non-linear data Excellent ✓ Good

Extra Trees uses randomly chosen split thresholds rather than searching for optimal ones — resulting in even more variance reduction and faster training, ideal for complex concrete mix interactions.
